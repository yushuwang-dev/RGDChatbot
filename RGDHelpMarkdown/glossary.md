# RGD Glossary of Terms

## Overview

The Rat Genome Database glossary provides definitions for genetics and bioinformatics terminology used throughout RGD documentation and database. Originally based on the Mouse Genome Database glossary, used with permission.

---

## Nucleic Acid Terminology

**3' (3-prime)** - Identifies one end of single-stranded nucleic acid molecules, terminating in a 3' hydroxyl group. Sequences are written with the 5' end left and 3' end right, reflecting the direction of DNA synthesis, RNA transcription, and mRNA translation.

**3' UTR** - The 3' Untranslated Region extending from the 3' end to the final codon used in translation.

**5' (5-prime)** - Identifies one end of a nucleic acid molecule, terminating in a 5' phosphate group.

**5' UTR** - The 5' Untranslated Region from the 5' end to the first translation codon.

**Adenine (A)** - A purine base component of nucleotides found in DNA and RNA.

**Base** - Nitrogenous compounds attached to the sugar-phosphate backbone in nucleic acids. DNA contains purines (adenine, guanine) and pyrimidines (cytosine, thymine); RNA contains purines and pyrimidines (cytosine, uracil).

**Base Pair** - The structure formed between complementary nucleotides via hydrogen bonding in double-stranded nucleic acids. Adenine pairs with thymine in DNA and uracil in RNA; cytosine pairs with guanine.

**Cytosine (C)** - A pyrimidine base component of nucleotides in DNA and RNA.

**Guanine (G)** - A purine base component of nucleotides in DNA and RNA.

**Thymine (T)** - A pyrimidine base component of nucleotides in DNA.

**Uracil (U)** - A pyrimidine base found in RNA (replacing thymine from DNA).

**Nucleotide** - An organic molecule comprising a nitrogenous base, a five-carbon sugar, and a phosphate group.

**Nucleic Acid** - A polymer of nucleotides; DNA or RNA.

**Purine** - A type of nitrogenous base with a two-ring structure (adenine, guanine).

**Pyrimidine** - A type of nitrogenous base with a one-ring structure (cytosine, thymine, uracil).

**Complementary Sequence** - A single-stranded nucleic acid that would bind to another via base pairing.

## DNA and Genetic Structure

**DNA** - Deoxyribonucleic acid, the nucleic acid comprising genes.

**cDNA** - Complementary DNA, a DNA copy of mRNA made using reverse transcriptase.

**DNA Construct** - An assembly of DNA sequences created in vitro for experimental purposes.

**DNA Methylation** - Attachment of methyl groups to DNA, typically at cytosine bases.

**DNA Polymerase** - An enzyme that catalyzes DNA replication.

**dNTP** - Deoxyribonucleotide triphosphate, referring to dATP, dCTP, dGTP, and dTTP.

**Codon** - Three bases in DNA or RNA sequences specifying an amino acid or termination signal (stop codon).

**Intron** - A gene sequence not present in mature mRNA after splicing.

**Exon** - A gene sequence present in mature mRNA after splicing.

**Coding Region** - The portion of a gene whose sequence is read as codons during translation.

---

## Gene Expression and Protein Synthesis

**Gene** - The basic unit of heredity; a DNA portion typically coding for a protein product. In RGD, this encompasses loci with altered phenotypes, necessary transcriptional units, hybridization-identified regions, exon-encoding segments, and intronic loci producing functional products.

**Gene Expression** - Transcriptional activity of a gene resulting in RNA and typically protein products.

**Gene Product** - A protein molecule produced by gene expression, or an RNA molecule not translated (tRNA, rRNA).

**Gene Symbol** - A unique abbreviation for a gene name.

**Gene Name** - A word or phrase uniquely identifying a gene, abbreviated by the gene symbol.

**Allele** - One variant form of a gene, differing in nucleotide sequence from other forms.

**Allele Symbol** - A unique designation for an allele variant.

**mRNA** - Messenger RNA, the RNA template for protein synthesis.

**RNA** - Ribonucleic acid, including mRNA, tRNA, and rRNA.

**tRNA** - Transfer RNA, transports amino acids during translation.

**rRNA** - Ribosomal RNA, a component of ribosomes.

**Transcription** - The copying of a gene into RNA.

**Translation** - Reading mRNA sequence information to assemble amino acids into proteins.

**Polyadenylation** - Addition of a poly(A) tail to mRNA.

**Splicing** - The removal of introns and joining of exons in RNA processing.

**Alternative Splicing** - Production of multiple distinct mRNAs from identical transcripts via splicing variations.

**RNA Polymerase** - An enzyme synthesizing RNA from a DNA template.

**Reverse Transcriptase** - An enzyme producing cDNA from an RNA template.

**EST** - Expressed Sequence Tag, a partial sequence of randomly chosen cDNA from a single sequencing reaction.

**Transcript** - An RNA copy of a gene.

**RNA Processing** - Post-transcriptional modifications converting transcripts to mature mRNA.

---

## Chromosomal and Genetic Organization

**Chromosome** - A structural unit within eukaryotic nuclei carrying genes, consisting of DNA and associated proteins.

**Centromere** - The primary constriction of a chromosome separating the short arm (p) and long arm (q). Rat chromosomes have centromeres close to one end with minimal short arms.

**Autosome** - Any chromosome that is not a sex chromosome.

**X Chromosome** - A sex chromosome; in mammals, females have two X chromosomes and males have one.

**Y Chromosome** - A sex chromosome; in mammals, males have one Y chromosome.

**Sex Chromosome** - Chromosomes determining biological sex (X and Y in mammals).

**Kinetochore** - The structure organizing over the centromere for chromosome movement during cell division.

**Heterochromatin** - Genomic regions with low gene density, highly repetitive sequences, and high condensation.

**Euchromatin** - Genomic regions with high gene density and relative absence of highly repetitive sequences.

**Chromatin** - Nuclear material comprising chromosomes, consisting of DNA and proteins.

**Chiasma** - The cytologically visible consequence of reciprocal recombination in meiosis.

## Genetic Variation and Mutations

**Mutation** - A change in DNA nucleotide sequence.

**Mutagen** - An agent causing mutations.

**Amorphic Mutation** - A mutation where the altered gene product lacks wild-type molecular function. Also called loss-of-function or null mutation.

**Antimorphic Mutation** - A mutation where the altered gene product acts antagonistically to wild-type alleles. Always dominant or semidominant.

**Gain-of-function Mutation** - A mutation where the altered gene product possesses new molecular function or expression pattern.

**Hypermorphic Mutation** - A mutation with increased gene product activity or expression.

**Hypomorphic Mutation** - A mutation with reduced gene product activity.

**Neomorphic Mutation** - A mutation producing an altered molecular function distinct from wild-type.

**Loss-of-function Mutation** - A mutation where the gene product lacks wild-type molecular function.

**Null Mutation** - A mutation eliminating gene product function.

**Deletion** - Loss of one or more nucleotides from a DNA segment.

**Duplication** - An additional copy of a DNA segment in the genome.

**Insertion** - Addition of nucleotides to a DNA segment.

**Inversion** - A chromosome rearrangement where a segment is reversed in orientation.

**Translocation** - Transfer of a chromosome segment to another chromosome.

**Transposition** - Movement of a DNA segment (transposon) to a new chromosomal location.

**Frameshift** - A mutation from insertion or deletion changing the reading frame.

**Reading Frame** - The grouping of nucleotides into sequential codons during translation.

**Chromosome Rearrangement** - A mutation changing the genome arrangement, visible cytogenetically.

**Chromosome Aberration** - Either a rearrangement or numerical aberration of chromosomes.

**Aneuploid** - Having an abnormal chromosome number.

**Euploid** - Having a chromosome number as an integral multiple of the haploid number.

---

## Genetic Variation Types

**Polymorphism** - The existence of multiple variant forms at a genetic locus.

**Marker** - A DNA segment used for genetic mapping and linkage analysis.

**STS** - Sequence Tagged Site, a short DNA sequence useful for mapping.

**Microsatellite** - A short tandem DNA repeat used as a polymorphic marker.

**Genetic Code** - The relationship of 64 nucleic acid codons to 20 primary amino acids.

---

## Genomic Organization and Mapping

**Genome** - The total genetic information of a cell or organelle.

**Genomics** - The comprehensive study of whole gene sets and their interactions.

**Genetic Map** - A map showing gene or marker positions on chromosomes.

**Linkage Map** - A genetic map based on recombination frequencies.

**Physical Map** - A genetic map based on physical DNA distances.

**Cytogenetic Map** - A genetic map relating gene positions to chromosomal banding patterns.

**Contig** - A physical map of contiguous genomic DNA from overlapping cloned segments.

**Synteny** - The occurrence of genes on the same chromosome.

**Conserved Synteny** - Synteny of orthologous genes in different organisms.

**Locus (pl. loci)** - A specific position on a chromosome.

**Centimorgan** - A genetic map unit; two loci are 1 cM apart if recombination occurs between them in 1% of meioses.

**BAC** - Bacterial Artificial Chromosome, a cloning vector carrying 100-200 kb of foreign DNA.

**YAC** - Yeast Artificial Chromosome, a cloning vector for larger DNA segments.

**Cosmid** - A cloning vector carrying about 40 kb of foreign DNA.

---

## Genetic and Phenotypic Relationships

**Dominant** - An allele producing the same phenotype in homozygous and heterozygous states.

**Recessive** - An allele showing its phenotype only in homozygous state.

**Codominant** - An allele where the heterozygote fully expresses both homozygote phenotypes.

**Semidominant** - An allele where the heterozygote phenotype is intermediate between homozygotes.

**Penetrance** - The proportion of individuals with a genotype showing the expected phenotype.

**Expressivity** - The relative constancy of phenotype for a given genotype.

**Haploinsufficient** - A gene producing mutant phenotype when heterozygous for a null allele.

**Epistasis** - Masking of a phenotypic trait through mutant allele action.

---

## Chromosomal and Cellular States

**Homozygous** - Having identical alleles at a locus (A/A).

**Heterozygous** - Having different alleles at a locus (A/a).

**Hemizygous** - A gene present in only one copy in a diploid cell (e.g., X-linked genes in males).

**Diploid** - Having twice the haploid chromosome number.

**Haploid** - Having the chromosome number of a gamete.

**Gamete** - A reproductive cell (sperm or egg).

---

## Genetic Crosses and Breeding

**Cross** - An experimental mating of genetically distinct organisms.

**Backcross** - Crossing a hybrid strain to a parental strain.

**Incross** - Crossing two F1 individuals from the same initial cross.

**Intercross** - Crossing two heterozygotes at the same locus.

**Outcross** - Crossing an individual to an unrelated individual.

**Testcross** - Crossing to a homozygous recessive individual.

**Hybrid** - Offspring of two homozygous individuals of different genotypes.

**Complementation** - Wild-type phenotype appearance in a hybrid of two mutant homozygotes (indicating mutations in different genes).

**Recombination** - Exchange of DNA sequences between chromosomes.

**Homologous Recombination** - Recombination between highly similar DNA sequences.

**Gene Conversion** - Nonreciprocal recombination where one allele is "converted" to another.

**Crossover** - A reciprocal recombination event.

**Linkage** - Tendency of genes to be inherited together due to chromosomal proximity.

---

## Strain and Stock Terminology

**Inbred Strain** - A strain produced by 20+ generations of brother-sister matings, achieving genome-wide homozygosity.

**Congenic** - An inbred strain containing a small genetic region from another strain, derived by 10+ generations of backcrossing.

**Coisogenic** - A strain differing from an inbred strain at only one locus, from mutation within that strain.

**Consomic** - An inbred strain containing an entire single chromosome from another strain.

**Conplastic** - Strains with the nuclear genome of one strain in the cytoplasm of another (mitochondrial parent always female).

---

## Cellular and Molecular Mechanisms

**Meiosis** - Cell division producing gametes, reducing chromosome number from diploid to haploid.

**Mitosis** - Cell division producing two genetically identical daughter cells.

**Mitochondria** - Cytoplasmic organelles with their own DNA, inherited maternally.

**Nucleus** - The membrane-bound organelle containing chromosomal DNA.

**Cytoplasm** - The cell portion outside the nucleus.

**Apoptosis** - Programmed cell death.

**Enzyme** - A protein (or rarely RNA) catalyzing chemical reactions.

**EC Number** - Standardized number assigned to enzyme types by the Enzyme Commission.

**Protein** - A macromolecule composed of amino acids.

**Amino Acid** - A molecule (NH2-CHR-COOH) serving as protein building blocks.

---

## Comparative Genomics

**Ortholog** - A gene related to one in another species by descent from a common ancestor.

**Paralog** - A gene related to another by duplication within the same genome.

**Homolog** - A gene or chromosome related by descent from common ancestral DNA.

**Evolutionary Conservation** - Presence of similar genes or chromosome segments in different species.

---

## Phenotype and Genotype

**Phenotype** - Observable characteristics determined by genotype and environment.

**Trait** - An observable characteristic or property.

**Genotype** - The genetic information carried by an organism.

**Gene Family** - A group of paralogous genes.

**Haplotype** - One of the alternative genotype forms of a gene complex.

---

## Ontology and Controlled Vocabularies

**Gene Ontology (GO)** - Controlled vocabularies describing biological features within knowledge domains.

**Controlled Vocabulary** - A restricted defined term set for complex information representation in databases.

**Biological Process** - A broad biological task accomplished via ordered molecular function assemblies.

**Cellular Component** - Subcellular structures, locations, and macromolecular complexes.

**Molecular Function** - Molecular-level activities describing biochemical activities.

**Hierarchy** - Organization with few things at the top and several below each.

**Ancestor** - A term any levels above another in a hierarchy.

**Parent** - A term directly above another in a hierarchy.

**Child** - A term below another in a hierarchy.

**Sibling** - Terms sharing a common parent.

## RGD-Specific Terms

**RGD** - Rat Genome Database; a comprehensive genetics information resource.

**RGD ID** - A unique alphanumeric string identifying database records.

**Accession ID** - A unique alphanumeric string identifying database records.

**QTL** - Quantitative Trait Locus; a genomic region associated with quantitative trait variation.

**Candidate Gene** - The likely causative gene for a phenotypic trait localized to a genomic region.

**Curated** - Literature articles reviewed by RGD curation staff who extracted and loaded information.

**Alias** - An alternative symbol or name for database objects (typically earlier publication names since updated).

**Object** - In RGD, any stored database item (gene, strain, reference, etc.).

**Nomenclature Event** - An event causing gene symbol or name updates.

**GeneWatch** - RGD's subscription feature for gene update notifications.

**Term Watch** - RGD's subscription feature for ontology term update notifications.

---

## Molecular Techniques

**Hybridization** - Formation of double-stranded nucleic acid via complementary base pairing.

**Probe** - A labeled nucleic acid segment for detecting complementary sequences.

**Southern Blot** - Technique detecting specific DNA sequences via hybridization.

**Northern Blot** - Technique detecting specific RNA sequences.

**Western Blot** - Technique detecting proteins using labeled antibodies.

**FISH** - Fluorescent in situ hybridization for cytogenetic localization.

**PCR** - Polymerase Chain Reaction, amplification of specific DNA sequences.

**RT-PCR** - Reverse Transcription PCR, amplifying RNA via cDNA synthesis.

**Electrophoresis** - Separation of charged molecules in an electrical field.

**BLAST** - Basic Local Alignment Search Tool, a sequence comparison algorithm.

**Sequencing** - Determining the order of nucleotides in DNA or RNA.

**Restriction Enzyme** - An endonuclease recognizing and cutting specific DNA sequences.

---

## Advanced Genetic Techniques

**Cre Recombinase** - A site-specific recombination enzyme recognizing 34 bp loxP sequences.

**loxP** - A 34 base pair sequence recognized by Cre recombinase.

**Floxed** - A DNA construct where a gene is flanked by loxP sites; Cre recombinase excises the segment between them.

**Gene Trap** - A DNA construct with a reporter gene downstream of a splice acceptor site, integrating randomly.

**Targeted Mutation** - A mutation deliberately created in a specific gene.

**Embryonic Stem (ES) Cells** - Undifferentiated embryo-derived cells capable of differentiating to multiple cell types.

**Chimera** - An animal from two different embryonic sources.

**Electroporation** - Using electric pulses to create temporary cell membrane holes for DNA introduction.

**Transposon** - A DNA segment capable of moving to new chromosomal locations.

**ZFN** - Zinc Finger Nuclease, a DNA-cutting protein with customizable specificity.

---

## Gene Regulation

**Central Dogma** - DNA makes RNA makes protein.

**Regulatory Element** - A DNA site affecting gene expression.

**Promoter** - A DNA region where RNA polymerase binds to initiate transcription.

**Enhancer** - A DNA site where transcription factors bind to affect gene expression.

**Splice Acceptor Site** - A DNA sequence where splicing joins exons.

---

## Database and Computing Terms

**Database** - An organized information collection storing metadata.

**FTP** - File Transfer Protocol; RGD's FTP site contains downloadable datasets.

**HTML** - Hypertext Markup Language for electronic documents.

**Algorithm** - A detailed action sequence performing a task.

**Boolean** - An expression evaluating to true or false (AND, OR, NOT operators).
